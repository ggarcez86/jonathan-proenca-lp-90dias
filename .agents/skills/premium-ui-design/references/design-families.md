# Design Families — Detailed Briefs

Three premium directions. Pick ONE as the dominant family. Each brief includes palette, typography, layout DNA, motion, and reference brands.

---

## Family 1 — Editorial

**Feeling:** Reading a long-form piece in a literary magazine. Serif-forward, asymmetric, confident with whitespace. The design gets out of the way so the ideas land.

**When to pick:** Expert is an author/thinker/public intellectual. Content is the product. Audience reads. Works for thought-leadership mentorships, non-fiction authors, keynote speakers.

### Palette
- Paper base: `#F7F4EF` or `#FAF8F3` (warm off-white, never pure white)
- Ink: `#14110F` or `#1A1613` (deep warm black, never pure black)
- Single muted accent: deep rust `#8B3A2F`, olive `#5C6343`, navy `#1E2A44`, or burgundy `#5C1E2B`
- No gradients. Solid colors only.

### Typography
- **Display:** Fraunces, PP Editorial New, Canela, Tiempos Headline, GT Super, Domaine Display
- **Body:** Untitled Sans, GT America, Söhne Breit, or classic humanist serif like Tiempos Text
- **Quote/pull:** Italic of the display serif, often oversized
- Size ratio: aggressive. H1 is 4–6x body size. No timid scaling.

### Layout DNA
- Asymmetric grids. Text column aligned left, images/quotes breaking into margin.
- Drop caps on opening paragraphs.
- Pull quotes as dominant visual elements.
- Wide margins. Content column max 680px.
- Dividers as thin hairlines, often off-center.
- Page numbers, section labels, running heads — editorial furniture.

### Motion
- Minimal. A subtle scroll-triggered fade for text blocks is enough.
- Consider a slow horizontal drift on decorative elements (quote marks, running numbers).
- No page-load fireworks. Restraint signals confidence.

### Reference brands
- The Browser, Aeon, The Atlantic (redesign), Are.na, Ghost marketing site, Freek van Heusden's portfolio, PP Editorial New specimen site.

### Capture form treatment
- Inline within the text flow, styled as a block quote or a sidebar.
- Labels in italic serif. Inputs with thin bottom-border only (no full box).
- Submit button: text-only or with a subtle underline, not a filled rectangle.

## Family 2 — Dark AI-forward

**Feeling:** Walking into a modern tech company's office at night. Deep blacks, precise typography, one electric accent, purposeful motion. Technology is the vocabulary — but refined, not flashy.

**When to pick:** Expert uses AI as methodology. Product/service has a technical core. Audience expects "moderno + sofisticado". **This is the primary recommendation for the Gustavo executive-mentorship-with-AI project.**

### Palette
- Background: `#0A0A0B`, `#0D0D10`, or `#08090C` (warm black, never `#000`)
- Surface 1: `#141417` or `#15161A`
- Surface 2: `#1E1F24` or `#212229`
- Border: `#2A2B32` or `#26272D`
- Text high: `#F4F4F5` or `#EDEDEF`
- Text mid: `#A1A1AA` or `#9B9BA3`
- Text low: `#52525B`
- **One accent**, picked deliberately:
  - Warm gold: `#C9A961` (luxury, executive)
  - Electric cyan: `#06B6D4` or `#22D3EE` (tech-forward)
  - Acid green: `#A3E635` (bold, young)
  - Burnt orange: `#EA580C` (warm, distinctive)
- Subtle gradient mesh OK on hero — but low-saturation, blurry, as atmosphere (not decoration). No rainbow.

### Typography
- **Display:** PP Neue Montreal, GT Alpina, Söhne Breit, PP Editorial New (for serif contrast), Migra, PP Object Sans
- **Body:** Geist, Söhne, GT America, Manrope
- **Mono (for technical flavor):** JetBrains Mono, Geist Mono, GT America Mono — use sparingly, as accent
- Size ratio: moderate-to-aggressive. Consider mixing a serif display against a geometric sans body for character.

### Layout DNA
- Split heroes (text left, visual right) — but break the pattern: overlap, asymmetric alignment, form floating over image.
- Sticky navigation with subtle blur backdrop.
- Sections separated by change in surface color (not by padding alone).
- Data/proof displayed as oversized numbers with mono-font units.
- Bento-style feature grids — but with varied card sizes, not uniform.
- Gradient mesh or noise texture as hero background, not as card decoration.

### Motion
- Orchestrated page load: nav fades in, hero text reveals in stagger, hero visual slides or zoom-in.
- Magnetic cursor on CTAs (subtle attraction within 40px).
- Scroll-triggered reveals with **y-translate + blur** (not just fade-up).
- Hover states: accent-colored underline slide-in on links, subtle elevation on CTAs (not big shadows — border brightening works better on dark).
- Consider a single "wow" moment: text that rewrites itself, a scroll-linked visualization, a gradient that shifts as you scroll.

### Reference brands
- Linear, Vercel, Arc, Raycast, Framer marketing, Mintlify, Cursor, Perplexity (pro), Anthropic, Rewind.ai, Granola.

### Capture form treatment
- Form as a distinct surface — lifted from background with surface color change + thin border (not shadow).
- Labels above inputs, small, uppercase, letter-spaced, in `text-mid`.
- Inputs on `Surface 2` with border on focus in accent color.
- Submit button: accent color background, dark text, full width of form, subtle hover scale (1.01) + border glow.

## Family 3 — Luxury Clean

**Feeling:** Walking into Aesop or reading a Loro Piana catalog. Quiet, warm, confident, restrained. Not trying to impress — it just is.

**When to pick:** Expert positions toward traditional luxury / old money. HNW audience. Aesthetic values over tech-forward signals. Works for premium legal practice, wealth management, luxury real estate, high-end concierge services. Also viable for executive mentorship targeting senior C-level with conservative taste.

### Palette
- Base: warm off-white `#F5F1EA`, `#EFEAE0`, or `#E8E2D5`
- Ink: warm black `#1F1B17` or deep espresso `#2B2520`
- Secondary: camel `#B8A384`, taupe `#9A8F7D`, stone `#C7BEB0`
- Single accent: muted forest `#3E4E3A`, deep wine `#56232B`, or navy `#22304A`
- No pure white. No pure black. No gradients. Paper/ink only.

### Typography
- **Display:** GT Sectra, Ogg, Canela, Garamond Premier, PP Editorial Old, Tiempos Headline
- **Body:** Maison Neue, Neue Haas Grotesk, Favorit, Untitled Sans
- **Small caps / labels:** Trajan, Copperplate, or letter-spaced body font in all caps
- Size ratio: moderate. Don't shout. Elegance is in proportion.

### Layout DNA
- Centered, symmetric layouts. Trust the center axis.
- Generous vertical spacing between sections — sections feel like chapters.
- Thin horizontal rules as section dividers.
- Hero: often a single oversized serif word or phrase, centered, with a subtle tagline below.
- Numbers written in small caps or roman numerals for chapter/step labels.
- Imagery when used: full-bleed, high-quality, often portrait orientation.

### Motion
- Almost none. A slow fade on page load. Gentle hover states.
- Restraint is the statement.

### Reference brands
- Aesop, Loro Piana, Brunello Cucinelli, Le Labo, Roman and Williams, Hermès Editorial, Bottega Veneta (current), old-money wealth management sites.

### Capture form treatment
- Form centered, narrow, with text elements styled as letterpress invitations.
- Labels as small caps below inputs (not above), letter-spaced.
- Submit button: bordered, not filled. Ink border on paper background. Text in small caps.
- Consider treating the form as a "RSVP" rather than a signup — language choice matters.

---

## Choosing between families

If you're genuinely unsure, produce a one-line pitch for each family in the context of the current project, then pick. Example:

> **Editorial:** Positions the expert as a thinker and storyteller. Risk: feels too literary for a workshop/webinar CTA flow.
>
> **Dark AI-forward:** Signals methodology + modernity. Risk: if expert's photography/assets are traditional, creates visual dissonance.
>
> **Luxury Clean:** Positions as old-money elite mentorship. Risk: may feel too conservative for an AI-focused methodology.

Pick the one whose risk is most acceptable for the project. State the choice and the reasoning before building.
